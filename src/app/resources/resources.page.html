The Dubai Big 4

Experience the iconic landmarks that define this remarkable city

1

Burj Khalifa

At 828 meters, the world's tallest building offers breathtaking views from its observation decks on floors 124, 125, and 148. Visit at sunset for the most spectacular experience as the city transforms from day to night.

Book tickets online in advance to skip the queues
2

Dubai Mall

More than just shopping—this mega-mall features over 1,200 stores, the Dubai Aquarium, an Olympic-sized ice rink, and the mesmerizing Dubai Fountain show outside.

Fountain shows run every 30 minutes from 6 PM
3

The Palm Jumeirah

This engineering marvel is visible from space—a man-made archipelago shaped like a palm tree. Home to luxury resorts and the famous Atlantis resort.

Visit The View at The Palm for 360° panoramic views
4

Al Fahidi Historical District

Step back in time in Old Dubai's preserved heritage site. Wander through narrow lanes, visit traditional wind-tower houses, art galleries, and the Dubai Museum.

Best visited in early morning or late afternoon

Holcim AMEA: Building Progress

Celebrating the diversity and impact of our Australia, Middle East & Africa regions.

Flagship Projects

Recycled Roads — Victoria, Australia

Holcim Australia leads circular-economy innovation by using recycled tyres in road base foundations, reducing waste and promoting sustainability.

Low-Carbon Concrete Tower — Dubai, UAE

Supply of ECOPact low-carbon concrete for sustainable high-rise developments in Dubai, lowering embodied carbon emissions and supporting green urban growth.

Near-Zero Cement Plant — Milaki, Algeria

Holcim El Djazaïr’s strategic expansion and port logistics strengthen exports to up to 40%.

Sustainable Build — Grand Egyptian Museum, Egypt

Holcim supplied sustainable materials for Africa’s first IFC EDGE Advanced Green Building certified site.

Water Infrastructure — Um Qasr, Iraq

Holcim supports infrastructure projects like the Common Seawater Supply Project near Um Qasr, part of Iraq’s integrated energy and utilities development.

Ebimpé Stadium Construction — Ivory Coast

Holcim provided 60,000 tonnes of cement for the first Olympic-sized stadium in Ivory Coast, a landmark for sports and national pride.

Proposed Cement Plant — Oamaru, New Zealand

Holcim plans a new cement plant in North Otago to expand presence, pending final regulatory approvals as of 2025.
